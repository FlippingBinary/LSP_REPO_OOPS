# Question 5 — Arthur Riel's Object-Oriented Design Heuristics

Heuristic 1:

Name:

Do not create god classes/objects in your system. (Heuristic 3.2)

Explanation:

You talked about this heuristic in terms of a class being responsible for only
one thing. We don't want mega-classes sprawling over everything. If it grows so
big that it becomes a god class, it probably is responsible for too much. I think
the example was thermostat-related.

Heuristic 2:

Name:

Model the real world whenever possible. (Heuristic 3.6)

Explanation:

You talked about this heuristic as a way to break down a god class into its corresponding
objects. Classes that are arbitrary can be difficult to maintain because the scope
of its responsibility becomes less clear.

Heuristic 3:

Eliminate irrelevant classes from your design. (Heuristic 3.7)

Explanation:

You talked about this heuristic as limiting the bloat and boilerplate of classes
for the sake of classes.
